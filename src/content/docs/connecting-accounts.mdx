Shoutrrr publishes through your own accounts. Bluesky works out of the box — no
developer app or credentials — thanks to ATProto OAuth. X and LinkedIn publish
through developer apps you register, so they need a few credentials in your
environment.

:::warning
Publishing and scheduling rely on a running queue worker and scheduler. The
provided Docker setups start both for you automatically.
:::

## Bluesky

Bluesky connects over **ATProto OAuth** — the recommended method — with nothing
to configure. There's no developer app to register and no client ID or secret to
set: Shoutrrr publishes its own OAuth client-metadata document from your
instance, and the DPoP signing key is generated and stored automatically on
first use.

From the **Connected accounts** screen, click **Connect Bluesky**, enter your
handle (e.g. `you.bsky.social`) or leave it blank to pick your account on
Bluesky, then authorize. To connect an account on a self-hosted PDS or another
ATProto service, open **Advanced** and set the **Service URL**.

:::note
OAuth requires your instance to be reachable at its real, public `APP_URL` over
HTTPS — Bluesky's authorization server fetches your client-metadata and JWKS
documents directly from that URL during the handshake. If you run behind a
reverse proxy, make sure the [HTTPS settings](/docs/configuration#reverse-proxies--https)
are correct so the generated `client_id` and callback URLs use `https://`.
:::

### App passwords (fallback)

If OAuth isn't an option, you can connect with a Bluesky
[app password](https://bsky.app/settings/app-passwords) instead — open **Use app
password** on the connect dialog. This is **not recommended**: app passwords
bypass two-factor authentication, and disconnecting the account in Shoutrrr does
not revoke the password on Bluesky — manage those from Bluesky's app-password
settings.

## X (Twitter)

Register an app at [developer.x.com](https://developer.x.com) and add its
credentials to your `.env`. The redirect URI must match what you register; it
defaults to `${APP_URL}/accounts/callback/x`.

```dotenv
X_CLIENT_ID=
X_CLIENT_SECRET=
X_REDIRECT_URI="${APP_URL}/accounts/callback/x"
```

## LinkedIn

Register an app at
[linkedin.com/developers](https://www.linkedin.com/developers) and add its
credentials. The redirect URI defaults to
`${APP_URL}/accounts/callback/linkedin`.

```dotenv
LINKEDIN_CLIENT_ID=
LINKEDIN_CLIENT_SECRET=
LINKEDIN_REDIRECT_URI="${APP_URL}/accounts/callback/linkedin"
```

## Optional: social login

Let people sign in with a social account instead of a password:

```dotenv
SOCIALITE_ENABLED=true
SOCIALITE_PROVIDERS=google            # comma-separated: google,x,linkedin
GOOGLE_CLIENT_ID=
GOOGLE_CLIENT_SECRET=
GOOGLE_REDIRECT_URI="${APP_URL}/auth/google/callback"
```

:::note
After editing `.env`, restart the container so the new values are picked up. Make
sure each redirect URI registered with the provider exactly matches your
`APP_URL`.
:::
